Web 1 Web 2 Web 3 Use case: query search for "side effects of medications"
Trust and Security Relies on centralized authorities for content verification, posing risks of misinformation and security breaches. Implements user feedback and moderation for trust-building, but still relies on central intermediaries for data management. Prioritizes trustlessness and security through decentralized architectures, leveraging blockchain for transparent, tamper-resistant transactions and data storage. Web1-Users must trust the credibility of static web pages managed by centralized entities, with potential risks of outdated or biased information. Web2- Trust is built through community feedback and platform moderation, but relies on central platforms to manage and secure data. Web3- Trust is ensured through decentralized blockchain technology, offering transparent, secure, and tamper-resistant access to verified information about medication side effects.
User Participation and Control Users passively consume content without contributing or controlling information. Empowers users to generate and share content, fostering community engagement and interaction. Gives users ownership of data and digital assets, allowing for selective sharing and control over personal information. Web1-Users can only read the information provided by website owners with no opportunity to add or modify content. Web2- Users actively contribute by sharing experiences and engaging in discussions about medication side effects on social platforms and forums. Web- Users control their own health data, choosing to share it selectively with trusted entities while accessing decentralized, user-verified information about medication side effects.
Technology Stack Uses basic HTML for static web pages with limited interactivity. Introduces dynamic content with JavaScript, AJAX, and APIs, enabling interactive user experiences. Leverages blockchain and decentralized technologies for secure, transparent, and peer-to-peer interactions. Web1- The search results would display static HTML pages with basic information about medication side effects. The content is text-heavy and non-interactive. Web2- Results would include dynamic, interactive content like user reviews, forums, and social media posts where people share their personal experiences with medication side effects. Personalized health apps might also provide tailored information. Web3- Search results would come from decentralized platforms with verified, tamper-proof data stored on blockchain. Users could access anonymized, trustworthy medical records and research studies securely.
